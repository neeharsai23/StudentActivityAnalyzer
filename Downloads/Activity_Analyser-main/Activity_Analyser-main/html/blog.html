While student online activity monitoring has proven effective at identifying at-risk students and aiding in potentially lifesaving situations some question if the use of this technology is an invasion of student privacy. The concern is that if schools are able to monitor students online activities, they could potentially use this information for unintended purposes. A Center for Democracy & Technology (CDT) study found that 57% of teachers and 61% of parents have concerns about student activity monitoring if its used or shared out of context or for disciplinary reasons.
